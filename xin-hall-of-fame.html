Xin's Hall of Fame

Xin's Hal l of Fame is a web-based platform that celebrates standout projects and top contributors within a collaborative community. Designed to highlight creativity, effort, and teamwork, it showcases user-submitted projects that meet criteria such as innovation, impact, and design quality. Each featured entry includes a project summary, contributor profiles, and visual highlights to inspire others and recognize excellence. The platform also encourages community growth by motivating participants to engage, collaborate, and strive for recognition through meaningful contributions.

Overview

As the Lead Developer and Designer for this 5-week project, I collaborated with a small team to design and build a fully functional website for Professor Xin Qin's research group at California State University, Long Beach. I led the frontend and backend development while working closely with Professor Qin to understand the group's goals, structure, and academic focus. The site was built to highlight ongoing research, showcase publications and team members, and provide an accessible platform for student outreach and collaboration. Throughout the project, I applied user-focused design principles, implemented responsive layouts, and ensured the site aligned with the professionalism of an academic research environment.

Xin Qin

CSULB

Xin qin is a Assistant Professor in the Department of Computer Engineering and Computer Science (CECS) at California State University, Long Beach (CSULB). Her research interests are in ensuring the safety of cyber-physical systems through formal methods, predictive monitoring, verification, and machine learning.

Role

As the lead developer and designer, I worked closely with Professor Xin Qin to create a research group website that accurately reflected her academic work and vision. I was responsible for building the site architecture, designing the user interface, and developing features to showcase publications, research projects, and team members. Throughout the process, I translated her goals into a responsive, accessible digital experience tailored for students, collaborators, and academic peers.

Research Group Needs

We began this project after meeting with Professor Xin Qin, who asked us to design and develop a website to represent her research group at California State University, Long Beach. The goal was to create a platform that showcased her team's work, made their publications and projects easily accessible, and helped attract new collaborators and student researchers. After our initial meeting, we reviewed her team's structure, ongoing projects, and key objectives to shape a clear direction for the site's purpose and features.

A Website Application

1 🧠 Showcase research projects, publications, and lab members
2 📬 Provide a clear way for students and collaborators to connect

User Needs

Based on our research and feedback from Professor Xin Qin's group, we identified key user needs for the new website. The platform should make it easy for students and collaborators to:

  • Discover ongoing research projects and publications
  • Connect with lab members and faculty
  • Access resources and updates relevant to their academic interests

Student
“I want to easily find research opportunities and connect with professors in my field.”
Collaborator
“I need a clear way to see the group's latest work and reach out for potential partnerships.”

Features

Bucket

The Bucket page serves as a collaborative hub where users can share and explore innovative ideas. It's designed to inspire creativity and foster community engagement by allowing users to contribute concepts, interact through comments, and potentially develop these ideas into full-fledged projects.

Projects and Publications

The Projects and Publications page showcases the group's research projects and published works. It provides a comprehensive overview of their academic achievements and research contributions, making it easy for users to explore and learn more about the group's work.The Projects & Publication page serves as a dynamic showcase of completed projects, allowing users to explore and learn from diverse initiatives across various disciplines.

Takeaway

Building this website for my professor was a rewarding and transformative journey. From the initial brainstorming sessions to the final deployment, I learned the importance of user-centered design, clear communication, and iterative problem-solving. Collaborating with my professor and understanding her vision for a platform that could serve future students and researchers pushed me to refine my technical and design skills. I'm proud that this site will be a resource for her group for years to come, and I hope it inspires creativity, collaboration, and innovation within the academic community.
